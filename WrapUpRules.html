Basic Chess Rules

Chess is played on an 8x8 square board with alternating light and dark squares. Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two bishops, two knights, and eight pawns.

How Pieces Move

King

The king moves one square in any direction. It's the most important piece - if your king is captured, you lose the game.

Queen

The queen is the most powerful piece. It can move any number of squares in a straight line - horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

Rook

The rook moves any number of squares in a straight line horizontally or vertically.

Bishop

The bishop moves any number of squares diagonally. Each bishop starts on a specific color and must stay on that color.

Knight

The knight moves in an L-shape: two squares in one direction, then one square perpendicular. Knights can "jump" over other pieces.

Pawn

Pawns move forward one square, but capture diagonally. On their first move, they can choose to move two squares forward.
