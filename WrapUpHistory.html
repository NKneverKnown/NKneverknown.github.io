History of Chess

Chess has a long and fascinating history that spans over 1500 years. The game we know today evolved from earlier games played in India, Persia, and Arabia.

Origins in India

The earliest form of chess originated in India around the 6th century AD. The game was called "chaturanga," which means "four divisions of the military" - infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariots, represented by the pawn, knight, bishop, and rook respectively.

Spread to Persia

From India, the game spread to Persia where it was called "shatranj." When Persia was conquered by Muslims in the 7th century, chess spread throughout the Islamic world and later into Europe.

Medieval Europeans playing chess

Modern Chess

The rules of chess evolved significantly in Europe between the 12th and 15th centuries. The queen and bishop gained their modern powerful moves during this period, making the game faster and more exciting.

The first official World Chess Championship was held in 1886, won by Wilhelm Steinitz. Today, chess is played by millions worldwide, both casually and in professional tournaments.

Interesting Facts

  • The longest possible chess game is 5,949 moves
  • The word "checkmate" comes from the Persian "shah mat" meaning "the king is dead"
  • The first chessboard with alternating light and dark squares appeared in Europe in 1090
  • There are over 600 million chess players worldwide
